What MVP1 is

MVP1 is a Commercial Persona workflow for "Just Say Yes" / "In The Know." It begins with SFDC intake, uses Opportunity Start Date as the mandatory gate date, checks whether the target start is more than four months out, asks for missing configuration data, calculates site/month options, and gives Commercial a green light or an off-ramp.

Do not change the scope: MVP1 does not reserve capacity, optimize room-level schedules, replace Central Scheduling, or create a final operational execution date.

Latest VoC synthesis

What Zach and the SFDC screenshots sharpened

Snapshot, not promise

PowerBI/RACE timing is useful, but it is perishable. Commercial needs validity language: eligible to propose, not reserved, recheck required after expiry or scope change.

Date semantics are risky

"Requested start" can mean in vivo start, LabSci method start, test material availability, or customer submission timing. MVP1 can use one gate date, but must expose ambiguity.

RFP already has data

RFP captures preferred site, triage site, requested start, management triage release, test material, LabSci triage, study design, and comments. The issue is synthesis, not absence.

Budgetary is different

Budgetary estimates can tolerate quarter or broad timing. Ready-to-execute requests and specific dates require stricter checks and may off-ramp to Central Scheduling.

Site flexibility is leverage

A single required site increases risk. A broader region or any qualified site can unlock earlier month options and should be first-class in the UI.

Free text still matters

Email, Teams, notes, and description fields hold constraints that rules may not parse. MVP1 should preserve a human-readable context capsule with every recommendation or escalation.

Current-state observations

What the screenshots imply

  • The Opportunity page is the commercial home base: stages, studies, RFP, files/SOW, Proposal Builder, notes, award, and health check.
  • The Study detail page already contains Scheduling Status values such as Timing Requested, Escalate Capabilities, Escalate Timing, Confirmed, Scheduling Expired, Requested Month Of, Confirmed Month Of, and Placeholder states.
  • Those status values are useful, but they currently mix recommendation state, timing precision, validity, off-ramp reason, and commitment level.
  • The Configurator and Study Support Component View contain rich operational signals, but MVP1 should use them to qualify and caveat Commercial recommendations, not to automate micro-scheduling.
Screenshots are not embedded in this public deployable package because they include current SFDC context and customer/opportunity examples. They were used as design reference only.

How to describe the MVP

Use this framing:

The Configurator and SFDC/RFP data give us enough structured study context to improve Commercial scheduling confidence. MVP1 should use that context to qualify, recommend, caveat, and escalate. It should not reserve capacity or override Scheduling judgment.
