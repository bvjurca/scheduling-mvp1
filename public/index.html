Proposal framing

What MVP1 should prove

The first release should let stakeholders validate the decision model and user workflow before committing to production integration depth. The strongest value is speed with traceability: a Client Manager can see why an option is safe to share, why it expires, and when Central Scheduling must take over.

In scope MVP1

  • Customer-requested commercial timing anchor known and more than four months out.
  • Required SFDC Opportunity, RFP, and Study configuration metadata present.
  • Site Capability and Lead Time / Site Availability checks.
  • Site / Month-of-Timing recommendation with expiration.
  • Central Scheduling triage reason and handoff context.

Out of scope Later

  • Exact date, room, or operations scheduling optimization.
  • Room Availability, Animal Availability, Test Item Receipt, and Reporting Milestones.
  • Labor, equipment, preferences, overbooking, profitability, and full production integrations.
  • Fully automated scheduling commitment.

Date language clarification

MVP1 should say "requested start / timing anchor" instead of bare "target date"

For this proposal, the gating date means the date or month the customer is asking Commercial to evaluate for the study. It may be captured in SFDC as Opportunity Start Date, Requested Start Date on the RFP, or Study timing context depending on the object. It should not be treated as a calculated LabSci lead-time date, test material availability date, room date, or RPM execution milestone.

Study record SFDC

Study records carry CRL Site, Scientific Group, Study Type, Product Line, Start Date, Anticipated Start Date, Anticipated End Date, and history. For MVP1 these are context, not a post-award execution system of record.

RACE modules Reference

The DSA Study & Animal Logistics dashboards already expose General Timing, Just Say Yes, LabSci Lead Times, TCR Lead Times, In Vivo Capabilities, and LabSci Capabilities. MVP1 can reference these patterns without absorbing all Release 2/3 constraints.

Stakeholder validation

Decisions this draft is designed to surface

What exact SFDC fields define sufficient configuration metadata?

Current placeholder: RFP requested start, preferred/triage site, Study Type 1, Study Type 2, species, and selected or preferred site.

What official label should replace ambiguous "target date"?

Recommended prototype language: requested start / commercial timing anchor.

What is the official rule when the customer does not know timing?

The model assumes a business-process placeholder start date may be needed, and that placeholder should be visibly labeled.

How long should a Site / Month-of-Timing offer remain valid?

Current prototype uses five business days from evaluation date.

What are the official Central Scheduling triage reason codes?

The wireframe includes missing data, too-soon start, site mismatch, lead-time issue, LabSci uncertainty, expired offer, and unresolved options.

What does "green light to say yes" legally and operationally mean?

This draft treats it as permission to share a site/month option, not a final operational commitment.

Who owns reference data after MVP1?

Site Capability, Lead Time / Site Availability, and Business Rules need named business owners and update cadence.
